# Keerthi Holdings / Keerthi PCB — verified facts (with APA sources)

> Research pass to make the sector-analysis section citable. **Bold corrections to my visit recollections are noted.**

## Safe to assert in the essay
- **Flexible (Flex) + rigid PCB manufacturer.** End-to-end capability: design → fabrication → finishing. Specialty finishes documented: ENIG, immersion silver/tin, HASL, **direct gold plating**; controlled-impedance (±10%); PTFE/Rogers high-frequency (RF) materials; up to 6 layers in mass production.
- **Certifications: ISO 9001:2015 + ISO 14001:2015 + UL-approved products.**
- **100% Export-Oriented Unit (EOU)** under the **Electronic Hardware Technology Park (EHTP) scheme**, Ministry of Electronics, GoI — since founding in **1992** (originally **Hyderabad Flextech Ltd.**, in technical collaboration with World Circuit Technology, USA — one of India's first flex-PCB makers).
- **Corporate history/structure:** merged into **Keerthi Industries Limited** (BSE-listed; cement-led, brand "Suvarna," Nalgonda, Telangana) in **2010** as its Electronics Division; the PCB operation now trades as **Keerthi Holdings Private Limited**. Both entities share the **Jasti family** promoters.
  - **Mr. Seshagiri Rao Jasti (J. S. Rao)** — MD & Chief Promoter of Keerthi Industries **and** MD of Keerthi Holdings. *(Likely the "MD" who entered our meeting.)*
  - **Dr. Venkata Krishna Jasti** — third-generation promoter (**Carnegie Mellon Ph.D.**), leads/guides the Electronics Division.
- **Location:** ~43,000 sq ft, Balanagar IDA, Hyderabad – 500037, Telangana.
- **Scale (small):** PCB/Electronics-division revenue ≈ **₹18.45 crore in FY24** (~US$2.2M). Listed parent Keerthi Industries ≈ ₹212 crore FY24, posted a **net loss** (~₹15.7 cr) and was rated **CARE BB; Negative** — i.e., the cement-led parent is financially stressed.

## EOU — what it means (for the analysis)
EOUs export their entire production in exchange for **duty-free import/procurement of capital goods and inputs**, and must achieve **positive Net Foreign Exchange over a 5-year block**. Governed by **Chapter 6 of India's Foreign Trade Policy** (DGFT, Dept. of Commerce). EHTP = the electronics-hardware variant.
