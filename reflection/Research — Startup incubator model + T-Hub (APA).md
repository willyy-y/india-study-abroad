# T-Hub / incubator model vs YC — verified briefing (APA)

## T-Hub facts
- **Founded 2015** (inaugurated Nov 2015); public–private partnership seeded by the **Government of Telangana** + **IIIT-Hyderabad, ISB, NALSAR.**
- **T-Hub 2.0** (opened 2022; phase-2 inaugurated June 2024): ~**572,000 sq ft**, 10 floors. T-Hub **describes itself** as one of the world's largest innovation facilities *(self-claim — attribute it).*
- Reports supporting **2,000+ startups**, who've raised **$2B+**.
- **Equity-free model (CONFIRMED):** T-Hub does **not** take a stake; monetizes via programs, corporate-innovation partnerships, sponsorships; some programs give **equity-free grants.** ⟵ genuine structural contrast with **YC (takes ~7% equity).** The visit framing was accurate.

## Successes / unicorns (honest finding)
- Best-attributable success: **Zenoti** (Hyderabad SaaS; unicorn ~2020–21, ~$1.5B). Also associated: MyGate, DrinkPrime, Outplay.
- **No large, cleanly attributable unicorn roster** like YC's — and that thinness **is itself a usable finding.** Lean on **aggregate** metrics (2,000+ startups, $2B+ raised), not a unicorn count.
- **Age comparison:** T-Hub founded 2015 (~11 yrs). YC founded **2005**; first headline unicorns ~**2011** (Airbnb ~$1.3B Jul 2011; Dropbox ~$4B 2011; Stripe ~$1.75B ~2014) → YC took ~6 yrs. T-Hub at the same age (~2021) had ~1 associated unicorn. YC today: **80+ unicorns, 17+ IPOs.**

## India "3rd-largest startup ecosystem" (verified — specify the metric)
- **Govt/DST framing:** India ranks **3rd globally** in startup ecosystem & unicorn count, **behind US & China**; DPIIT recognizes **150,000+ startups** (early 2025).
- **Tracxn (2025) funding framing:** 3rd-largest **tech** ecosystem by funding in 9M2025 (**$7.7B**, ~−23% YoY), **behind US & UK.** *(Different "top two" — name which you mean.)*

## "$11B deep-tech fund / 7 yrs" — CONFLATED (correct this)
- **Deep Tech Fund of Funds (Budget 2025):** **₹10,000 crore ≈ $1.1B**, via SIDBI. *(One outlet literally headlined it "$1.1 billion.")*
- **Original Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS, 2016):** ₹10,000 crore, SIDBI, sector-agnostic.
- **RDI Scheme (approved Jul 1, 2025):** **₹1 lakh crore (~$11–12B) over ~6 yrs**, includes a deep-tech FoF. ⟵ this is where the "$11B" likely came from (decimal/conflation).
- **National Deep Tech Startup Policy (NDTSP):** a policy, not a fund.
- ✅ **Use precisely:** either "~$1.1B Deep Tech Fund of Funds (2025)" **or** "~₹1 lakh crore RDI scheme (~$11–12B, 2025) which seeds a deep-tech fund." Don't merge.

## YC (for the comparison)
- Founded **2005**; ~3-month batches; **standard deal $500k** ($125k for ~7% + $375k uncapped SAFE); **for-profit, equity-taking.** Alumni: Airbnb, Stripe, DoorDash, Coinbase, Dropbox, Reddit.

## Essay use
- T-Hub (equity-free, state-anchored) + DVC (returnee-founder VC) = the **"new entrepreneurship"** counterweight to Keerthi's **old family capital** — but both still rest on *elite* inputs (state subsidy, US-tech networks). The honest thesis: India is **broadening** entrepreneurial access, not yet democratizing it.
