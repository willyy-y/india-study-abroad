# Make in India — the measurement-critique argument (APA)

> **My thesis:** judging the policy by *manufacturing as a raw % of GDP* (stuck ~15–17%, target 25%) is the **wrong yardstick.** Judge it on **engineering employability** + **second-order industrial deepening** (supplier ecosystems, exports, skills, China+1 capture).

## The most powerful, well-sourced triad (lead with this)
1. **~80% of Indian engineers are "unemployable"** for the knowledge economy (Aspiring Minds, 2019; "stubborn unemployability," unchanged since 2010). ⟵ the human-capital metric that matters.
2. **India became a *net exporter* of mobile phones** and the world's #2 smartphone maker; PLI delivered real deepening in specific value chains (PIB, 2025; IBEF/ICEA, 2024).
3. **Dani Rodrik — premature deindustrialization** (2016): developing countries hit peak manufacturing at *lower* income/employment shares than early industrializers; India began deindustrializing at ~$2,000/capita. ⟵ principled reason the 25% target was misconceived.

## Point 1 — Goals vs. results
- Launched **25 Sep 2014** (DPIIT). Targets: manufacturing **16%→25% of GDP by 2022** (later 2025); **12–14%/yr** mfg growth; **100M new manufacturing jobs by 2022**.
- **GDP share stagnated/fell** by every series — direction robust, level series-dependent: World Bank current-price ≈ **17% (2010) → ~13% (2022–23)**; constant-price GVA ≈ **17–18%**. ⚠️ *State which series you cite.*
- **Jobs target missed.** CMIE/CEDA: manufacturing employment **~51M (2016–17) → ~27.3M (2020–21), ≈ −46%** ⚠️ *(private data, COVID-affected — cite as CMIE/CEDA, not govt; pull PLFS for an official number).*

## Point 2 — PLI scheme (strong primary source: PIB 2025)
- 14 sectors, outlay **₹1.97 lakh crore**. As of 2025: 764 approvals; **₹1.61 lakh cr investment**; **~₹14 lakh cr production**; **exports >₹5.31 lakh cr**; **~1.15M jobs**; **60% import substitution in telecom**; India now a **net mobile-phone exporter.**
- Mobile production **US$49.16 bn FY24** (~21x in a decade); mobile **exports ~US$14.44 bn FY24** (IBEF/ICEA, 2024).
- **Defense exports: record ₹23,622 cr FY2024–25** (+12% YoY); up from ₹686 cr in FY2016–17 (PIB/MoD, 2025).

## Point 3 — Employability (my strongest evidence)
- **Aspiring Minds NER 2019:** >**80% unemployable**; ~3.84% employable in software; ~3% have new-age (AI/ML/data) skills. 170k+ grads, 750+ colleges. ⚠️ *Private firm (now SHL) — name its commercial interest for fairness; data still heavily cited.*
- **Aspiring Minds NER 2016:** only ~**7.5%** employable in core engineering roles.
- **Wheebox India Skills Report 2024:** overall youth employability **~51%**.
- **ILO India Employment Report 2024** — authoritative for skills mismatch / youth employment (cite directly for the manufacturing skills-gap claim).
- ⟶ **Ties to Keerthi:** its binding constraint was *finding and keeping capable engineers* — the micro evidence for the macro skills gap.

## Point 4 — Why % of GDP is a poor metric
- **Rodrik (2016, 2015):** premature deindustrialization; services-led growth can't easily substitute (high-productivity IT doesn't employ the low-skilled at scale). India's mfg employment peaked ~13% (~2002) then fell.
- **Jobless growth:** employment elasticity fell ~1.0 (1970s) → ~0.4 (1990s) → <0.1 recently ⚠️ *anchor to ILO/IHD primary, not coaching-site summaries*. Mfg growth increasingly **capital-intensive**.
- **Fairness counter (include):** ORF argues "jobless growth" is overstated.
- **Better indicators (= my "second-order effects"):** export sophistication, domestic value-added, component-ecosystem depth, productivity (KLEMS), job *quality* (PLFS misses security/benefits).

## Point 5 — China+1: capturing it?
- India assembles ~¼ of global iPhone output; Apple ~55M iPhones in India in 2025 (up from ~36M in 2024) ⚠️ *(analyst/press, not govt)*.
- **Honest caveat (= my thesis):** India captures **assembly/labor**, not yet the **high-value component ecosystem** (most components still China). Real deepening is partial.
