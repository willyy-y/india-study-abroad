# Scavenger hunt — the 26-item cultural-immersion checklist

> The instructor's hunt: "two weeks, three cities, one phone." Photograph items, then explain *what it tells you about India.* Presented at the May 24 debrief. **This is the best scaffold for the "skills / new ways of seeing" prompt** — concrete, specific, photographed (what the rubric wants).

## The categories (and items I hit organically — link to my notes)
**Food & experience:** chai from a street cart · **street food I couldn't ID before eating** (✓ [[05-13 — Elephanta Caves]] food tour) · **a thali, all components photographed** (✓ Dakshin, [[05-20 — DVC + Microsoft]]) · **food prepped in a way that wouldn't pass Western inspection but I ate anyway** (✓ the "MADE WITH BISLERI" pani-puri, [[05-13 — Elephanta Caves]]).

**Urban life & culture:** religious image at a place of business · **3+ forms of transport in one photo** · **cricket** (✓ [[05-12 — Mumbai city tour]]) · auto-rickshaw carrying something absurd · sign in a language I can't read · **an animal doing its business in the city, unbothered** (✓ monkeys / camel).

**History & the city:** **100-yr-old building with a modern business inside** · one detail that says *which city* · **British colonial remnant still in daily use** (✓ CST/Taj hotel, [[05-12 — Mumbai city tour]]) · the most crowded space · Bollywood poster.

**India's economy in action:** **UPI/QR payment in the most informal setting** (✓ everywhere) · **informal economy doing what a formal business would need an office/contract/machine for** (✓ dabbawalas) · a "Made in India" label that surprised me · one link in a supply chain (✓ Keerthi / dabbawalas) · **evidence of the middle class** (distinctly Indian + distinctly modern).

**Development & technology:** **solar panels in an unexpected spot** (✓ ties to [[Ornate Solar]]) · infrastructure being built · tech ambition in the wild (IT campus / incubator / 5G next to something old — ✓ [[T-Hub]]).

**Wildcard:** billboard mixing two languages · graffiti with a legible message · **#26: "something you cannot explain — come with a question, not an answer"** (worth the most).

## Why it matters for the essay
- **The hunt trained a *way of seeing*.** By week 2 I was reading the street as an economy and a culture (order-in-chaos, informal economy, UPI penetration, colonial layering) rather than just sightseeing. That shift **is** the answer to the skills prompt.
- **Item #26 = the rubric's "raise questions" value**, embodied by my recurring **Islamic-heritage / Hindu-majority** open question (Taj, Mecca Masjid, Golconda).
- Concrete photographed items = the **specificity** the rubric demands; pick 2–3 vivid ones for the essay.
