# Mumbai Dabbawalas

## About
Century-old logistics network delivering ~200,000 home-cooked meals daily across Mumbai via local trains and bicycles. Famously studied at Harvard Business School for its near-zero error rate.

## Notes from visit
> I'd presented on the Dabbawalas the day before (deck: `India Dabbawala Presentation.pptx`; workspace: [[Mumbai Dabbawalas — Presentation/_index|Presentation hub]]). Seeing it live vs. the slides:
- **Biggest surprise:** most dabbas are **no longer the iconic gray tiffins** — they're ordinary lunchboxes with the **coding written directly on the box**, mostly **packed by the worker's family** (often mother or wife). The romantic image is already half-gone.
- **The system in motion:** arrived to lunchboxes piled on the ground; within ~**20 minutes** everything was **sorted and loaded onto bikes/carriers** and the dabbawalas rolled off on their routes.
- **Tourism as a side-business:** a Chinese YouTuber was filming; they offer a **"follow one lunchbox" full tour** (potential extra revenue). Guide spoke polished English, clearly well-practiced.
- **MAIN TAKEAWAY:** culture and tradition are **deeply embedded** — they don't need modern tech because the **existing system already works** (North's **Q2**: informal norms + social enforcement substituting for formal IT).

## Takeaways
- **"Tradition lives in the method, not the object"** — the most-photographed symbol (the gray tiffin) is disappearing while the *system* persists. The small tension a tidy framework would miss.
- A clean **North Q2** example to pair with HDFC's **Q1** (formal-but-weakly-enforced).
