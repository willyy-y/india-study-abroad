## Notes from visit
**Weak visit — but usable as contrast.** Main takeaways on the Indian telecom market:
- A **few large players.** **Jio** made **data super cheap** (the price disruptor). **Airtel** is the **"premium"** service. Plus **tail-end suppliers + government-subsidized telcos** serving **rural** communities.
- **Nokia's role:** builds the **physical infrastructure** running the network; also makes **datacenter hardware (leaf-and-spine switches).**
- **The tell that it fell flat:** robotics posters with the Nokia logo on the walls → I asked *"is Nokia entering robotics hardware?"* → **"No."** So why the posters? Little real insight.

## Takeaways
- Confirms the **Jio-as-disruptor** story from Class 2 (cheap data = a leapfrog enabler).
- A **low-information visit** — useful only as a foil to the strong ones (Ornate Solar, MS-PM). Reinforces my "speaker/prep quality determines value" observation.
