## Notes from visit
**What Ornate actually is:** NOT a solar-panel manufacturer. They **engineer and install a roofing solution that uses solar panels *as* the roof** (an all-in-one solar + roof). Benefit: **better coverage and higher space efficiency** than panels mounted on top of a conventional roof.

**Most of the visit = a TAM / market-sizing of Indian renewables** (this is the valuable part). Figures *(flag for APA verification):*
- India's solar output grew from **~2.8 GW → ~155 GW in ~10 years.**
- Current grid: **~525 GW installed capacity**; estimates say India needs **~800–900 GW.**
- **Solar is ~1/5 as effective as coal** (weather, sun-uptime) **but cheaper long-run** — the cost is essentially a **one-time setup fee.**
- As **electricity transmission costs rise**, users shift toward **self-sustaining (behind-the-meter) generation** — keep what you generate, consume as needed, avoid an unstable grid at peak.
- **Indian demand peaks *twice* daily** (morning pre-work + evening) vs. the typical Western **single** daily peak. *(Nice cultural/infrastructure contrast.)*

**Adoption barriers (the tension):** individual **storage capacity**; **roof repairs require whole new panels**; hard to **retrofit old buildings** with the all-in-one solar-roof tech.

## Takeaways
- **Huge potential market, real structural barriers** — a clean "framework would miss this" tension (storage, retrofit, repair economics).
- **Best speaker of the trip tier.** Speaker quality >> company prestige in what I actually learned.
