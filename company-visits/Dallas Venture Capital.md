## Notes from visit
- **Who:** Dallas-based VC that **operates and invests in India.** Founders are **Indian, with prior careers in US tech.** Run **two separate funds** (one India, one US).
- **Sector thesis:** ~**50/50 infrastructure** (GPU efficiency, cloud, storage) **and application** (agentic AI). Next year: lean more into **deep tech + hard manufacturing.**
- **The Hyderabad shift they described:** city used to be **back-office heavy** (ML training, image labeling, automation). As **US-tech Indians moved back home** (lower cost of living, family), India began building **home-grown product.** Plus the govt pushing the **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) stack** and a deep-tech fund. ⟶ India is now the **3rd-largest startup ecosystem in the world.** ⚠️ **CORRECTION (see [[Research — Startup incubator model + T-Hub (APA)]]):** the "$11B / 7 yrs" recollection is conflated — real figures are the **~$1.1B (₹10,000 cr) Deep Tech Fund of Funds (2025)** plus the separate **~₹1 lakh crore (~$11–12B) RDI scheme**. "3rd largest" verified (specify metric).
- **4 drivers of the ecosystem:** (1) US product workers returning home; (2) fintech boom + DPI; (3) big tech setting up in India; (4) SaaS boom 2010–15. **Next:** AI enterprise + deep tech.
- **Investing lessons they shared:** don't invest too early; deploy **sizeable** capital — especially in manufacturing where **CAPEX is higher.**
- **4 founder attributes they look for:** (1) technical understanding/skills; (2) financially savvy (don't run out of money); (3) discipline + communication; (4) an eye for talent. *(Personal: these echo Paul Graham / YC essays I've been reading.)*
- **Portfolio:** ~**30 companies, all enterprise software.** Enter **Series A–C**, target **$1–5M**, **10–20% stake**; **10 companies** in the India fund.

## Takeaways
- DVC is the **professionalized, funded face of "new" Indian entrepreneurship** — BUT the founders are **US-tech returnees**, a globally-connected elite. So it's **not wealth-independent meritocracy**; it's a *different* form of privileged capital (human/network capital vs Keerthi's old money). **This nuance is gold for the mobility cultural-comparison** — it complicates a tidy "India is democratizing entrepreneurship" story.
- Their pivot toward **deep tech + hard manufacturing** intersects directly with the Make-in-India / Keerthi thread.
