# T-Hub

> **Surprise/bonus visit** — the [[Dallas Venture Capital]] partner called a T-Hub contact and walked us across the street. The **"new entrepreneurship" counter-image** to [[Keerthi Holdings]]' old-family-capital model.

## What it is
- Startup **incubator + coworking space** in Hyderabad; connects startups with members/sponsors and **distributes cloud-compute credits** — every startup gets **~$50k each in GCP, Azure, and AWS** credits from sponsor companies.
- **Unlike YC in the West, T-Hub does NOT take equity or keep profits.** (Equity-free, ecosystem/government-and-sponsor-backed model.)
- **Google for Startups** sits in the same building. Toured the facility — felt **very "googley"** (open, polished startup-campus vibe). A **product demo room** let us touch some of the tech.

## The guide's "3 failure nodes" for startups
1. **Building in stealth** that doesn't actually solve a problem.
2. **Running out of money** fast.
3. **AI eating your lunch** — frontier labs ship features so fast they can erase a startup's edge.

## Why it matters for the essay
- **Equity-free, infrastructure-provision incubator** = a structurally *different* model from US venture/accelerators.
- Reinforces the **mobility tension**: alongside DVC, it's the institutional push toward **wealth-independent entrepreneurship** — the optimistic counterweight to "you need old money" (Keerthi). The essay should weigh both.
