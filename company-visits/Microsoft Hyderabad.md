## Notes from visit
**Talk 1 — 20+ year Product Manager (strong).**
- Microsoft enterprise vs consumer; **enterprise is the largest revenue driver, Azure the powerhouse.** Discussed **greenfield** processes (things that didn't previously exist).
- **My question:** PM is metrics-driven — how has AI changed *which* metrics matter / moved the benchmarks? **Answer:** time & money are the biggest AI indicators; measure the **delta** (pre-AI vs post-AI time/money saved). For **greenfield**, you must invent a baseline. *(Found this last part confusing / not sure I agree → **open question**, but going deeper would've exceeded class scope.)*
- **PM in the AI era:** now expected to (1) design/solve problems AND (2) **build light demos themselves** rather than wait on full eng teams. *(Reminded me of Anthropic/Snap-style "free time to build" engineering culture — unsure if MS has that, but it's an AI-accelerated industry shift.)*
- **Azure is the backbone of UPI.** *(ties Microsoft into the DPI/fintech story.)*
- **Regional advertising in India** (cultural gem): India = many subcultures, so ad targeting differs by region. Framework: (1) keep the **core global**, (2) **adapt the experience locally**, (3) **tell stories that feel native.** ⟶ good **cultural-comparison** color on India's internal diversity.

**Talk 2 — "Cloud Solutions Architect" (5 yrs @ MS, 8 total) — weak.**
- Gave a **template deck on how students can use AI** — my own workflows are already more advanced.
- **My question:** does the enterprise **data/information layer need to be rebuilt around agents** (a chatbot lacks the company context/visibility a human has)? **He couldn't answer.** Also couldn't explain how MS helps big companies integrate AI beyond the Copilot extension/app.
- **My read:** Microsoft is **well-positioned at the OS level to solve the enterprise data-ontology layer** but hasn't shipped a real solution — and this speaker had no view on it.

**After — AI-stack chat with Dr. Srini (good).** He sent his **GenAI-stack whitepaper** → reviewed in [[Research — GenAI stack whitepaper (Dr. Srini)]]. **Takeaway, upgraded:** my first read was *"companies must post-train models to their business."* The whitepaper sharpens this — the *intuition* is right, but the *mechanism* is mostly the **data/retrieval layer (L3) + context engineering (L5)**; true fine-tuning is the **expensive last resort** ("exhaust prompt and context engineering first"). The paper also names **L3** as exactly the **"rebuild the data layer around agents"** question I asked the weak speaker. *(Use this nuance — it's the citable, sophisticated version.)*

## Takeaways
- Microsoft = the **"global big tech operating in India"** data point; **Azure ⟷ UPI** links it to the DPI growth story.
- The **quality gap between the two speakers** is itself a usable observation about depth-of-bench.
- The **regional-advertising** model is a crisp, citable-from-the-visit illustration of India's internal cultural heterogeneity (use in cultural-comparison).
