# ECGC (Export Credit Guarantee Corporation of India)

## About
Government of India enterprise (est. 1957) under the Ministry of Commerce & Industry. Provides export credit risk insurance to Indian exporters.

## Notes from visit
- A government **export-credit agency**: provides **payment insurance** to Indian exporters — "a guarantee of compensation for specific incidents in return for a premium." Hedges **commercial and political risk** from missed/late payment.
- **Structure:** **short-term** cover (payment < 1 yr — most physical goods) vs. **long-term** (1–15 yrs — infrastructure / overseas construction).
- **Worked example (the memorable bit):** India builds an airport in a poorer country (e.g., Zimbabwe), funded by an Indian bank; if the local government defaults, ECGC insures the developer and **India can take control of the airport** → export insurance as **economic statecraft / soft power.**
- Drier than the Dabbawalas, but not a dud.

## Takeaways
- Another data point for **"the state has a role in helping a developing market thrive"** (cf. [[Class 2 Notes]]) — here, **subsidizing/insuring exports** to push goods and services outward.
