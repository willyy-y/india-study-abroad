## Notes from visit
> Not a study of HDFC per se — the visit was an **economic primer** for the whole course (see [[05-14 — HDFC Capital + Classroom]]).
- HDFC = India's largest private bank; **HDFC Capital** is its real-estate fund-manager subsidiary (raises third-party funds for housing development).
- **KEY TAKEAWAY — the "enablers" accelerating India toward a self-sustaining economy:**
  - *Tech:* government **proof-of-identity (Aadhaar)** + cheap data + cheap smartphones → **UPI**, which democratizes finance and banks the previously unbanked (≈ Cash App / Venmo).
  - *Cultural:* **self-reliance** + **frugal workarounds (jugaad)** + a **growing startup culture** (→ [[T-Hub]], [[Dallas Venture Capital]]).
- **SURPRISING — how young India's financial-product market is:** only **one life-insurance company until 2000** (LIC monopoly); **mortgages** popular only ~25 years; the **legal/regulatory infrastructure** lags — courts have **thousands of backlogged cases, multi-year waits**, so complex lending is hard to enforce. *(Direct illustration of North: weak* enforcement *of formal rules.)*

## Takeaways
- The visit set up the trip's spine: **formal rules vs. informal/weak enforcement** (the court backlog) and **state-built digital rails** (Aadhaar/UPI) echoing the **China playbook.**
- Open question I left with: why didn't Cash App/Venmo explode in the US the way UPI did here? (Long-standing banks + universal accounts.)
