# Keerthi Holdings

> **Actual host for the May 18 "Manufacturing in India" session.** Supersedes the pre-trip placeholders [[Cyient]] and [[Granules India]], neither of which was visited. **Primary candidate for the framework / sector-analysis section.**

> ⚠️ **VERIFIED FACTS + APA sources now live in [[Research — Keerthi (verified facts + APA)]].** Key corrections: **AS9100 is NOT confirmed (treat as not held); named customers (Philips/J&J) are NOT citable — use "medical, automotive, defense" instead.** Structure confirmed: founded 1992 as Hyderabad Flextech (100% EOU/EHTP) → merged into listed, cement-led Keerthi Industries (2010) → PCB now Keerthi Holdings Pvt Ltd; **Jasti family** promoters; **MD = Seshagiri Rao Jasti**; 3rd-gen **Dr. Venkata Krishna Jasti (CMU PhD)** runs electronics. PCB-division revenue ≈ ₹18 cr FY24 (small). Theory backbone (Khanna & Palepu institutional voids + North) in [[Research — Family business groups vs US PE (APA)]].

## The business
- **Rigid and flex PCB manufacturer.** In-house: fab shop, CNC, clean room, plating (incl. gold plating), etc. End-to-end **R&D + design → fabrication → finishing** under one roof.
- **Certs:** ISO 9001:2015 + ISO 14001:2015 + UL. **AS9100 (aerospace): they do NOT hold it but are *actively working toward* certification** — part of a deliberate strategy: more certs → wider customer base. (Good analysis beat: cert acquisition as a growth lever.)
- **Key customers:** **Philips and J&J MedTech were named by the company during our visit** (cite as a visit observation / personal communication, since not publicly documented online). Broader documented framing: medical, automotive, defense.
- **100% Export-Oriented Unit (EOU):** gets government support for exports via **tax waivers**. Can also sell **domestically (defense)** but must pay tax on those sales.
- Walking tour of the factory — saw products and machines running.
- **Rare capability:** one of the only firms in India with **flex PCB** capability.

## Holding structure (the central tension)
- Confusing in research: **Keerthi Industries** (publicly traded; includes a **cement** business) vs. **Keerthi Holdings** (private holding company). Appear to be owned by the **same family**. ⟶ *[subagent verifying]*
- Broader pattern: like **Tata Group** and the **Adani** family, **concentrated family wealth ends up owning many companies across unrelated industries.**
- **Contrast with US private equity**, where investors/operators specialize in an **industry vertical**. Indian model = diversified family conglomerate; US model = focused specialization. ⟶ *[subagent researching, APA]*

## The MD moment (power distance, live)
- The MD entered partway through; **Prof. Srini stood up immediately** to address him. Sharply dressed — read as **"old money" on vibes alone.**
- When he asked questions, he **directed them at Prof. Srini, not the students** (we'd probably have been too intimidated to answer anyway).
- **Interpretation:** textbook **Hofstede power distance**. And a bigger realization: in India, **only the already-wealthy can afford to start a capex-heavy business** like PCB manufacturing → **less broad-based entrepreneurship than the US.**

## Government / policy
- **Make in India** is a huge driver of domestic manufacturing.
- Specific policy mentioned: **~25% CAPEX kickback/subsidy** → funds better machines. ⟶ *[APA: verify which scheme — PLI / state capital subsidy.]*
- On the floor: CNCs ~**$60k**, a laser cutter ~**$400k** (≈$100k to repair/refresh).
- **EOU status** waives export taxes.
- **Constraint — talent:** hard to **find** engineers capable of the work, and hard to **retain** them once hired. Possibly an **education-system** issue → ties back to the **"40% of grads unemployable"** thread (HDFC day).

## Rocket Lab lens (my firsthand comparison)
- At **Rocket Lab** I saw additive-manufactured engines, space structures, satellite clean rooms, advanced tube bending.
- **Surprise at Keerthi:** the sheer machinery/capability packed into an **unassuming Hyderabad building** — full PCB process end-to-end (design → fab → gold plating). Advanced manufacturing where you'd least expect it.
