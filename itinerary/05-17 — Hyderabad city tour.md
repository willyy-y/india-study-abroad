## Notes
**Hyderabad city tour.**

**Golconda Fort (highlight).** Climbed it around 10am and it was already blazing — I finished a full water bottle in the parking lot *before* even entering. **Islamic heritage** is heavy here.
- **Locals kept asking our group for photos**, then following us for a few minutes afterward — they seemed *more interested in us than the fort.* (Goals-prompt payoff: my pre-trip fear was racism/hostility toward East Asians; what I actually got was *curiosity and friendliness*. The fear inverted.)

**Old city — Mecca Masjid (Makkah Masjid, near Charminar)** [verify spelling/date in citation pass] **+ the bazaar (Laad Bazaar).**
- Had **Irani chai** at the bazaar.
- **Bargained** a "**Rolec**" (not Rolex) watch down to **200 rupees** — didn't buy. Also bought **pearls** (Hyderabad is famous for them) at a jewelry store, and even "established" stores have negotiation room. **You can negotiate on *everything* here.** (Cultural-comparison thread: price as a starting point for relationship/haggling vs. fixed-price US retail.)

**OPEN QUESTION (flag for essay — genuine, unresolved):** the architecture shows deep **Islamic heritage**, yet modern India is majority **Hindu**, and there's visible **religious tension in politics** between the groups. How did that shift happen? I know little about this and don't want to over-claim — so the essay should *raise it as an honest open question the trip surfaced*, not answer it. (Rubric rewards "raising questions.")

**Evening.** Rode a **rickshaw for the first time** to a liquor store for wine — a blast. After that we started taking **Uber auto-rickshaws** as much as possible (Uber is the only way to avoid being overcharged).
