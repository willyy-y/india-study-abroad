## Notes

**Morning class (Class 2 session).**
- Quick **recap of the trip so far** and how visits tied back to classroom topics.
- **Lewis model** of development (agriculture → manufacturing → services) and how **India ran it in reverse**: services-led, with **manufacturing stuck at 15–17% of GDP for 30+ years.**
- That fed a **structured debate: "Is Make in India working?"** Groups randomly assigned — **I had to argue the FOR side** (policy acting as intended).
  - *My assigned group's stance:* manufacturing's GDP share is **stable while GDP grows aggressively (~6–8%)**, so manufacturing is **growing in absolute terms** even if its share hasn't moved. No winner was declared.

**My actual personal view (use this — it's the honest take):** I think the policy is **NOT really working** — or at least, **the metrics being used to judge it (raw % of GDP) don't capture what matters.** Better measures would be **university engineering-program placement/employability outcomes** and **second-order effects** of a deeper manufacturing base (supplier ecosystems, skills, wages), not the headline GDP share.
- *Ties to Keerthi (prev day):* a real EOU leaning on the Make-in-India CAPEX subsidy, yet its binding constraint was **finding and keeping capable engineers** — exactly the employability/skills gap. The factory floor confirmed the macro doubt.
- ⟶ research subagent dispatched to get citable data on this (see [[Research — Make in India (APA)]]).

**Afternoon — Taj Falaknuma Palace, high tea.** Former palace of the ultra-rich (Nizam era); blazing hot; **3 Michelin Keys.** Not much analysis, but a vivid marker of India's wealth strata. *Personal note: I'll come back to actually stay here one day.*

**Evening — the "Forget Me Not" shisha adventure (PERSONAL HIGHLIGHT + thematic payoff).**
Shisha lounges are **commercially banned in Hyderabad**, so I used Claude to find spots. Drew (who wanted it most) was skeptical any would actually serve. With Angela, we took a **25-minute rickshaw ride** to "Forget Me Not Cafe" and got dropped **in front of a men's hostel.** Around the corner: a **cage elevator guarded by some men.** We rode it to **floor 5**, stepped out into **shisha smoke and fruity smells** — it was real.
- **Why it's a highlight:** experienced shisha culture *and* it **cemented the trip's recurring theme that "laws are merely suggestions"** (same as the road rules on day 1 — informal norms override formal rules; North's framework, lived). I was **proud of committing to the adventure** — riding 25 minutes into the unknown not even sure the destination existed.
- **Skills / ways-of-seeing beat:** comfort with ambiguity + committing on incomplete information. A pre-trip goal was getting *out of my comfort zone* — this is the proof.
